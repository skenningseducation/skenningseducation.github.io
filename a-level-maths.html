A Level Maths

A structured A Level Maths programme for Years 12–13, built around fluency, reasoning, and exam technique. Suitable as supplementary support or as a complete teaching pathway.

Included from Day 1: lesson notes (Word/PDF), exam-style questions with worked solutions, and access to past papers for the student’s exam board. Maximum class size: 10 students.

What we focus on

  • Strong algebra and calculus foundations
  • Clear, efficient problem-solving methods
  • Exam technique and mark allocation awareness
  • Regular mixed practice to improve retrieval and speed

Typical 30-week outline (support programme)

Exact sequence depends on the student’s specification (and whether they do Mechanics or Statistics).

Weeks Main topics Exam focus
1–6 Algebra & functions: indices/logs, quadratics, graphs, transformations, composite/inverse Method selection, accuracy and presentation
7–12 Calculus: differentiation + applications, integration basics Interpretation questions, modelling
13–18 Trigonometry & exponentials: identities, equations, radians, trig graphs Proof/justify style responses
19–24 Year 13 core: harder integration, sequences/series, numerical methods (spec dependent) Multi-step questions, structure under pressure
25–27 Applied (Mechanics/Statistics): key models and high-frequency exam topics Interpreting context, clear working, units
28–30 Exam phase: timed papers, targeted weak-area rebuilds, mixed-topic drills Timing, exam strategy, mark scheme habits

Who this is for

  • Supplementary students: weekly support to stay on track and improve grades.
  • Main provision students: complete teaching pathway with structured notes and exam practice.
