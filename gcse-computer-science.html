GCSE/iGCSE Computer Science

A structured programme covering theory and programming, with regular exam practice. Suitable as supplementary support alongside school or as a complete Computer Science pathway.

Included from Day 1: full lesson notes (Word/PDF), exam-style questions with worked answers, and access to past papers for the student’s exam board. Maximum class size: 10 students.

What we focus on

  • Clear understanding of the full GCSE/iGCSE theory content
  • Programming in Python (building confidence and accuracy under time pressure)
  • Exam technique: command words, structure, mark-scheme language
  • Regular recall and practice so knowledge sticks

How lessons run

Weekly live teaching

  • Teacher-led explanation and worked examples
  • Guided coding and problem solving
  • Regular checks for understanding
  • Short exam-style questions every week

Homework + feedback

  • Weekly independent practice
  • Exam-style questions with model answers
  • Targeted improvements to gain marks
  • Timed practice to build speed and confidence

Typical 30-week overview (Sept to exams)

Exact ordering is adapted to the exam board and class needs, while keeping full coverage.

Weeks Focus Outcome
1–6 Foundations: systems basics, CPU/memory/storage, data units, core concepts Strong baseline understanding and accurate core explanations
7–12 Data representation + algorithms (tracing, pseudocode, common patterns) Reliable methods and fewer mistakes in exam-style problems
13–18 Programming fundamentals (Python): variables, selection, iteration, lists, functions Confident coding and structured solutions
19–24 Networks, cybersecurity, logic, databases (as required by exam board) Clear theory answers and improved application to scenarios
25–27 Programming depth + exam patterns (timed tasks and typical question types) Faster coding, stronger accuracy under exam timing
28–30 Exam phase: timed papers + targeted weak-area rebuild Better timing, mark-scheme technique, exam confidence
