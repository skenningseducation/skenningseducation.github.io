A Level Business Studies

A focused A Level Business Studies programme built around case studies, data response, and exam-focused evaluation. Students learn how to build high-mark answers with clear judgement, strong context and effective chains of reasoning. Suitable as supplementary support or as a complete teaching pathway.

Maximum class size: 10 Case studies + data response Evaluation technique built-in
Included from Day 1: full lesson notes (Word/PDF), exam-style questions with worked answers, and access to past papers for the student’s exam board.

How lessons run

  • Live weekly lesson: teacher-led explanation, worked examples, and guided application.
  • Data response practice: interpreting figures, selecting evidence, and drawing justified conclusions.
  • Evaluation training: build chains of reasoning, weigh trade-offs, and reach a clear judgement.
  • Exam technique: planning quickly, writing to the mark scheme, and timing under pressure.
  • Feedback: precise improvements focused on gaining marks.

30-week overview plan (Sept to exams)

General structure. Exact ordering is adjusted by exam board and class needs.

Weeks Main focus Typical outcomes
1–5 Business objectives, stakeholders, strategy basics, interpreting context Cleaner application to case studies and stronger written structure
6–10 Marketing strategy: segmentation, positioning, product/price decisions, market data Better context-based justification and evidence selection
11–15 Finance + decision-making: profitability, cash flow, investment appraisal (as required), ratios More accurate calculations and stronger “what the numbers mean” explanations
16–20 Operations + competitiveness: efficiency, quality, supply chains, capacity, change management More confident evaluation using constraints and trade-offs
21–25 People + leadership: culture, motivation, HR strategy, organisational design Stronger analysis of management decisions with clear judgement
26–30 Revision + timed papers + high-mark evaluation technique Exam readiness, improved timing, and consistently higher evaluation marks
