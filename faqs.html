FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about how Skennings Education works for GCSE/iGCSE and A Level students.

If you’re unsure which subject/level is the best fit, send a quick enquiry and we’ll advise.
Is this for supplementary support or can it be the student’s main provision?

Both. Many students join for extra support alongside school. Others use this as their main teaching provision (for example, where school provision is limited or the student needs a clearer structured pathway). We adapt homework and pace to suit the student’s context.

Which subjects do you teach?

We teach Computer Science, Maths and English at GCSE/iGCSE and A Level.

What exam boards do you cover?

We cover major UK exam boards. On Day 1 students receive structured notes and exam-style practice that is clearly mapped to the major boards, and we flag what is specific to the student’s board.

How big are the classes?

Maximum class size is 10. This keeps teaching interactive and allows regular checks for understanding.

How do lessons run?

Lessons are live and teacher-led. Typically: explanation, worked examples, guided practice, then exam-style questions. Students also receive structured practice between sessions, and feedback focuses on gaining marks.

Do you set homework and give feedback?

Yes. Students receive targeted tasks between lessons. Feedback is designed to be clear and actionable: what to fix, how to fix it, and what will gain marks in the exam.

Do you provide notes and past papers?

Yes. Students receive a complete set of lesson notes in Word/PDF. We also provide access to past papers for their exam board.

How do you place students into the right group?

We place students by year group and current confidence so the pace is right. If needed, we’ll ask a few short questions (or a quick baseline task) to confirm the best fit.

Do you help with exam booking?

We can advise parents on common routes for private candidates (for example, centres that accept external candidates), but bookings are completed by the parent/guardian with the chosen centre.

What results or qualification outcomes do you guarantee?

We don’t guarantee specific grades — outcomes depend on the student’s starting point, attendance, and effort. What we do guarantee is a structured plan, consistent teaching, and exam-focused practice that builds confidence and performance.
